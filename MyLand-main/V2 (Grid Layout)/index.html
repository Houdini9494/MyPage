About Me

I'm Giacomo and I've always loved computers and IT in general.
My interests began when I was five years old, when I used my grandfather's computer for the first time; the operating system was Windows 95, which I later upgraded to Windows 98 SE.
This early experience sparked a curiosity in me that led me to find out everything I could about hardware and how computers work.
At the age of 15, I received my first desktop computer, which marked the beginning of my journey into experimenting and learning.
I taught myself to assemble, modify, and repair computers and notebooks, building solid technical skills that I’ve continued to develop and refine.
Over time, my passion expanded beyond hardware.
During the 2020 lockdown, while staying at home like so many others, I seized the opportunity to dive into programming.
I began studying HTML and CSS to create web pages, and this initial step opened up a whole new world for me.
In 2021, I decided to pursue a professional path in software development and soon resumed my academic studies to further enhance my knowledge.

Since the end of 2022, I’ve been working towards my degree in Computer Science, focusing on improving my skills in languages like Python, C/C++, SQL, and more.
I’ve also developed a strong interest in cybersecurity, and I’m eager to make it my area of specialization.
Currently, In addition to my studies, I'm also balancing a couple of jobs to support myself financially; I work as an assistant baker and also manage online sales for a bookstore.
It’s definitely a challenge to balance work and school, but I’m fully committed and determined to make it all work.
My love for technology drives me and I’m excited to learn more and more; I'm really hoping to land a job in the IT field so I can apply what I've learned.
I aim to graduate by 2025, after which I want to dive into a master’s degree in cybersecurity to deepen my knowledge and skills.
My curiosity and excitement for innovation keep me motivated, and I'm ready to seize any opportunities that come my way in the IT world, especially focused on cybersecurity and software development.
